Hi, I'm Hung, a fourth year Math major at the University of Chicago. I'm currently interested in the theory of deep learning! My thoughts are recently inspired by:
1. [Misha Belkin's](http://misha.belkin-wang.org/) research directions, and his blog posts on the necessity of developing a deep learning theory.
2. [Rebecca Willett's](https://willett.psd.uchicago.edu/research/) motivation for her research directions.
3. The book [*The Principles of Deep Learning Theory*](https://deeplearningtheory.com/), whose philosophy of an "effective theory" is worth pursuing, but I wonder if the assumptions are sufficiently realistic to reflect empirical success.

Besides all this, I am also interested in reinforcement learning and its theoretical foundations. Because I love robots. There are not as many "inspirations" here as there are above, but I do think a lot of future success in RL hinges on the simpler DL case.
